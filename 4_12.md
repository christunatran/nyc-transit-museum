### other things to look into
- jointly operated IRT and BMT trains across Astoria and Flushing
    - how was ridership counted for those rows?
    - page 5 footnote from `1930-1995-Ridership.pdf`: "(a) for passenger fare collections at all entrances of this station, see page 149. ticket sales here shown on astoria and flushing branches represent net sales for the interborough rapid transit company after deducting portion allocated to the new york rapid transit corporation on account of joint traffic over these routes."
    - reason why its IRT (not BMT) for joint-operation stations: under Dual Contracts of 1913, infrastructure ownership: Astoria and Flushing lines east of Queensborough Plaza were officially IRT lines under Contract 3. BMT held "irrevocable and equal trackage rights" but the lines themselves were IRT's -- the IRT paid for contraction, leased the infrastructure from the city.
        - => each row in our data represent's IRT's net revenue share from that station. BMT's share is on pages 149/151 of the source document and represents BMT's allocation credit form the same fare pool

        The Transit Commission's accounting followed the contract, not the train. At a joint station:
- how transit commission accounted the jointly operated lines:
1. A passenger paid 5¢
2. The fare entered a shared pool
3. A proportionate formula allocated the revenue — most to IRT (the lessee), a negotiated portion to BMT (the trackage-rights holder)
4. IRT's share was published in the IRT section of the annual report (page 5 in the 1930s PDF)
5. BMT's share was published separately (pages 149/151, not in your PDF)
